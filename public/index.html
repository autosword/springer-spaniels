A love letter with receipts

Springer Spaniels:
Adorable Chaos Machines

They have the face of an angel, the energy of a toddler on Red Bull, and absolutely zero remorse. This is their story.

🐾 💥 🐾

Their Most Wanted List

Documented offences. All confirmed. No remorse shown.

The Mud Obsession

They can detect a puddle from 400 metres. They will detour 20 minutes off-route to locate it. They will roll in it. Then they find you specifically to share the experience.

Selective Deafness

Can hear a biscuit wrapper from two floors up. Cannot hear "NO", "COME", "STOP", or "PLEASE GOD DON'T EAT THAT" under any circumstances.

Counter Surfing

They have the arm span of a basketball player and the ambition of a startup founder. Nothing on your kitchen counter is safe. The audacity is breathtaking — they make eye contact while doing it.

3am Zoomies

Scientists have studied this. No one understands it. One moment: sleeping peacefully. Next: full-speed laps around the house, rebounding off furniture, vibrating at a frequency that wakes the neighbors.

The Shoe Situation

They don't chew both shoes. That would be too practical. They take one shoe — always one — to some location that will never be found. This is not destruction. This is art.

Sock Theft Ring

They do not eat the socks. They collect them. They have a hoard. They are running an underground sock economy and you are simply not privy to the exchange rate.

Rolling in Gross Stuff

Fox poo. Dead fish. Something unidentifiable. They find these things. They treasure them. They marinate in them. Then they look at you with pure joy because they want to share this gift with you.

The "I'm Starving" Performance

They were fed 20 minutes ago. And yet — somehow — they are now performing a death scene worthy of a BAFTA, flopped dramatically across the kitchen floor.

They're Lucky They're Cute

Evidence A through F in the ongoing case for their defence.

Look at them. Charges: dropped. Every time.

The Springer Spaniel Survival Guide

Hard-won wisdom from people who have been through it.

1.

Accept that you are not the alpha. You are the obstacle.

The sooner you stop trying to "train" them and start negotiating, the happier everyone will be. You have leverage (treats). Use it wisely.

2.

Never make eye contact during the zoomies.

Eye contact is an invitation. It escalates. Stand very still and wait for the storm to pass. Do not speak. Do not react. You are a tree.

3.

Own two of every shoe. Keep one pair in a locked vault.

This is not paranoia. This is infrastructure. Think of it as shoe redundancy. The shoe you are wearing right now is being assessed.

4.

Exercise them into a stupor. Every. Single. Day.

An under-exercised Springer is a force of pure chaos. Two hours of running is not enough. It is a warm-up. They were bred to hunt all day. Plan accordingly.

5.

Budget for the "incident fund."

Something will be destroyed. Something will be eaten that shouldn't be. Something will require a vet visit that begins with "so, funny story." Set money aside. It's not if. It's when.

6.

Remember: they love you unconditionally, immediately, and forever.

After every crime, every muddy paw on white jeans, every sock heist, every 3am sprint — they look at you with those ridiculous eyes and you forgive everything immediately. This is their superpower. They know it.

Still worth it. 10/10. Would adopt again.

Made with love, mild exasperation, and dog hair on every surface.
