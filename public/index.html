Their Top Crimes

All charges confirmed by multiple witnesses. All charges subsequently dropped because they looked up with those eyes.

💧
Charge #1

Mud Diving

A Springer does not merely walk through mud — they commune with it. They can locate a single puddle in an otherwise bone-dry park through means that science cannot explain. They will return to you looking like a swamp creature wearing your dog's face, wagging furiously. You just bathed them two hours ago. They know this.

🔇
Charge #2

Selective Deafness

Springer Spaniels can hear a crisp packet open from three floors away, a fridge door open through a closed window, or the word "walk" whispered under your breath. "COME HERE" when they're sniffing something unspeakable? That frequency simply does not register. Scientists are baffled. The dogs are not.

🍗
Charge #3

Counter Surfing

Your Springer has not stolen food from the counter. They have redistributed it. The roast chicken you left to rest? Redistributed. The Christmas ham? Also redistributed. They view the kitchen as a communal resource and your objections as purely advisory. They make eye contact while doing it.

🌀
Charge #4

3am Zoomies

Without warning. Without reason. Possibly at 3:17am on a Tuesday. Your Springer will achieve velocities previously unmeasured in domestic mammals, rebounding off furniture in physics-defying figure-eights. It lasts 90 seconds. They sleep for 14 hours after. You will never understand it. You were not meant to.

🧦
Charge #5

The Sock Economy

They don't eat the socks. They collect them. They have a hoard. In a location that will never be found. They are running an underground sock economy and you are simply not privy to the exchange rate. Every sock you own will eventually end up in the hoard. This is non-negotiable.

💀
Charge #6

Rolling in Dead Things

Fox poo. Dead worm. Something unidentifiable that may predate the Roman Empire. They find these things. They treasure them. They apply them with the focused dedication of someone putting on expensive cologne. Then they look up at you with pure, unbridled joy — because they want to share this gift. They love you that much.

The Springer Spaniel
Survival Guide

What they don't tell you in the breed description.

1

Accept that the mud is non-negotiable.

You can avoid puddles, take only paved routes, and wrap your dog in plastic wrap. None of this will work. There will be mud. Embrace it. Buy a good hose.

2

Nothing on the counter is truly safe.

If it is within reach of an outstretched nose, it is at risk. If it smells like food, it is at severe risk. Move your whole life to above 1.5 metres or make peace with the redistribution.

3

Keep one shoe in a locked safe at all times.

They won't destroy both shoes. Just one. Always one. But which one will be taken is unknowable. Maintain a minimum reserve of one secure shoe per pair so you're never fully stranded.

4

Exercise is not optional. It is survival.

An unwalked Springer is a Springer with time on their hands and ideas in their head. Walk them. Run them. Field them. Whatever it takes to convert that energy into tired happiness rather than creative destruction.

5

When they look at you with those eyes — you will forgive them.

This is not a weakness. This is the whole point. The chaos, the mud, the sock hoard — all of it is a setup for those moments where you realize this ridiculous animal loves you with absolutely everything it has. Worth it.
